Routine Servicing

Prolonging the life of your vehicle

Your vehicles servicing manual will advise you when your vehicle should be serviced, this is usually measured in months or miles and it will generally recommend an interim or a full service as regular intervals.

During an interim service, the following could be checked:

Bodywork & Mirrors, Timing Belt Interval, ABS, Warning Lights, Seatbelts, Lights, Wipers and Washers, Fuel Cap, Aircon (Where Required), Power Steering, Aux & Fan Belts, Air Filters, Battery, Clutch, Hand Brake Operation, Gear Box Oil, Fluid Levels, Corrosion Checks, Engine Oil Replaced, Oil Filter Replaced, Steering & Suspension, Tyres, Brake Pads, Drums & Discs

A full service could also include:

Door Hinges catches and locks, Coolant System, Cooling Fan, Throttle Operation, Air Filters Replaced, Condition of Distributor Cap, Checks for Engine Mounts Wear, and various other visual checks.

Exactly what is carried out will be determined by your manufacturers guide, and as such prices will vary from make to make and model to model - however having your vehicle regularly serviced can vastly improve its reliability and lifespan, call us today for a quotation.
